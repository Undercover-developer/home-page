Oyindamola Koleoso

Full Narrative Bio

Oyindamola Koleoso is a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Dallas, where he is a member of the Smart Cities Applied Research Lab (SCALA Lab). His research focuses on multi-agent systems, distributed intelligence, and the development of scalable, data-driven infrastructures for smart cities. Oyindamola is passionate about building autonomous systems that enable intelligent collaboration among agents, with the goal of advancing the next generation of urban technologies.

Prior to his doctoral studies, Oyindamola earned his M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Arkansas at Little Rock, where he worked under the supervision of Prof. Nitin Agarwal. His master’s research explored the intersection of social computing and large-scale data analysis, contributing to projects that leveraged machine learning and network science to understand online communities and information diffusion.

Oyindamola’s technical expertise spans a wide range of areas, including distributed systems, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and data engineering. He has hands-on experience with technologies such as Python, Java, Golang, React, Node.js, Apache Spark, and Kubernetes. His work in both academic and industry settings has involved designing robust software architectures, developing real-time analytics platforms, and deploying scalable solutions on cloud infrastructure.

In addition to his research, Oyindamola has contributed to several open-source projects and has a keen interest in the practical applications of AI and distributed systems. He has published in peer-reviewed conferences and journals, including work on improved clustering for textual data and emotion-centric analysis of hyperlinks in blogs. His recent projects include the design of a peer-to-peer distributed storage system (D-CAS) and a cloud visibility dashboard for infrastructure monitoring and optimization.

Oyindamola is also dedicated to teaching and mentorship. He has served as a teaching assistant and guest lecturer for courses in software engineering, database systems, and social computing. He enjoys helping students develop strong foundations in computer science and guiding them through hands-on projects that bridge theory and practice.

Outside of academia, Oyindamola is an advocate for technology-driven social impact and is actively involved in initiatives that promote STEM education and digital literacy. He believes in the power of technology to transform communities and is committed to using his skills to drive positive change.
